Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. 

A common example involves emails or messages that look like they are from banks, delivery services, or popular apps.
In a typical phishing attack, the victim first receives a message that appears urgent or important, such as “Your bank account will be blocked—click here to verify.” The message includes a fake link that looks real.
When the user clicks the link, they are taken to a fake website that closely resembles the official one. The victim then enters their login details or financial information. 
Once submitted, this data goes directly to the attacker, who can misuse it for fraud.

Students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify links carefully. People who are new to digital banking are also common targets.
The consequences can be serious. Victims may lose money, have their accounts hacked, or face identity theft. Recovering from such attacks can take time and may also cause stress and loss of trust in online systems.
Phishing continues to grow because it relies on human mistakes rather than technical weaknesses.
